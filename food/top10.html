Top 10 budget places to eat in Delhi.

Posted on June 19, 2017

​ Hey loves, It’s been a happy weekend for me and I am pumped up for the coming week as I’ve got a lot in store for you.

I, being the biggest foodie alive on earth, wants to try every now and then something new. Some of the eatables are so delicious that you couldn’t resist but go there again and again and have that piece. Today I’ve come up with this amazing list of Budget restaurants to fill your heart and keep you pocket intact.

1. La Americana, Connaught Place



Where: Connaught Place and Netaji Subhash Place. Must try : All the premium burgers and Over The Top (O.T.T) French fries. Remember the O.T.T is really spicy so you should take some non-spicy burger with it.

Cost: ₹450 for two.

2. Starbucks



Where: Its outlets are all over Delhi, so you can pick a place where you’re comfortable. Must try: EVERYTHING! (Though my favourites include Java Choco Chip and Red Velvet Cake) Cost: ₹600 for two.

3. Billu’s Hut



Where: It is situated at Netaji subhash place. Must try: Oreo Shake, Maggi Pasta, Red sauce pasta, White sauce pasta.

Cost: ₹350 for two.

4. Rajma Chawal Point



Where: Connaught place is the hub of good food, it is in Shankar Market.

Must try: It serves kadhi chawal, chole bhature with lassi and many more. (But my favourite is Rajma Chawal) See how delicious it is. Cost: ₹150 for two.

5. Nazeer



Where: It is many outlets, find out which is nearby you.

Must try: Chicken Masala Biryani,Mutton Kebab Roll, Chicken Tandoori with Butter. Cost: ₹600 for two.

6. Haldiram



Where: Many outlets.

Must try: Though I’m not a very vegetarian type of person when I go out but Raj kachori of Haldiram took my heart away. And you can try all kind of North Indian and South Indian Food there. Delicious! Cost: ₹550 for two

7. Oye Kathi Rolls



Where: It is situated near Nirman Vihar metro station, at a distance of 10 mins of walking. Must try: The best chicken lollypop in this town. Cost: ₹160 for two.

8. Indian Coffee House



Where: 2nd Floor, Mohan Singh Place, Connaught Place. Must try: Hot Cream Coffee, Cheese omelette and Cold Cream Coffee. Cost: ₹200 for two.

9. Gulshan Dhaba



Where: It is in outskirts of Delhi, situated in Murthal. And what is better than a long drive plus yummy food.

Must try: Aloo ka Paratha with Lassi. Cost: ₹300 for two.

10. Naturals Ice Cream



Where: After all these meals we all need some desert to complete it all. Many outlets, one in CP. Must try: Any ice-cream can work but my favourite is Choco Bite Ice-cream. Cost: ₹130 for two.

Ishanki Tiwari
